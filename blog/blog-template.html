Sept. 14

The big chunk of meat our cohort has had to chew on (and digest) this week is the concept of "Enumerable."" The Enumerable module provides a collection of methods we can apply to iterate on Ruby's collection classes, in order to search, sort and manipulate them. It's a bit hard to penetrate at first, but it saves a tremendous amount of time if you learn to use them well.

There are myriads methods inside the Enumerable class, the most popuar ones being: Each, each_with_index, map or collect, chunk, partition, select, and inject.
What makes it even more flexible, one can combine methods, such as map and each: if you chain the each_with_index method with map, you can have access to the current index inside your map block.
